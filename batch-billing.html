Redesigning a Batch Invoicing Experience

Challenge

On one hand we have users that are trying to lighten their workload but find the feature lacking assumed functionality and call in to find the help they need not reducing their workload. On the other hand we have customers that are new to the product and are trying to explore the feature and find it overwhelming or do not find it and call in to find the help they need. How can we help both sides maximize use of the feature while remaining efficient for current users with expanded functionality and accessibility for new users so that they are not overwhelmed? This solution aims to address both sides of this user problem.

Approach

I worked closely with front facing teams to determine the framework and layout for the feature and each page. Working with the team in SD I shared iterations via Invision to get input and comments from them. I pulled a lot of user data from a long list of Observational/ Usability calls that I had made to find patterns in requested functionality.

Outcome

I am currently working with our Senior UX Designer and team to get this feature updated and have the new functionality implemented.
